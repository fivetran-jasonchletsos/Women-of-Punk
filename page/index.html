Memoirs, oral histories, criticism, and the zine cuttings that actually told the truth at the time.

BOOKS

2010

Just Kids

by Patti Smith

Patti Smith and Robert Mapplethorpe, broke and in love in pre-yuppie New York, becoming the artists they became. Won the National Book Award. The starting point.

2015

M Train

by Patti Smith

A book about cafés, dreams, dead writers' graves, and Patti Smith's rented Detroit bungalow. The opposite of Just Kids — a quieter, weirder masterpiece.

2017

Devotion

by Patti Smith

Slim Why I Write lecture turned into a book about writing, with a short story embedded in it. The most useful thing Smith has written on the act of making art.

2019

Year of the Monkey

by Patti Smith

A grief memoir in dream form. Smith on tour, watching Sam Shepard die, and writing through the first year of the Trump administration.

2024

Rebel Girl: My Life as a Feminist Punk

by Kathleen Hanna

Kathleen Hanna's memoir, published by Ecco. Bikini Kill, Le Tigre, the Julie Ruin, Lyme disease, and the inside story of "girls to the front" — finally on the record.

2010

Girls to the Front: The True Story of the Riot Grrrl Revolution

by Sara Marcus

The definitive narrative history of riot grrrl. Marcus interviewed everyone and built a chronology nobody had bothered to assemble. Read this before anything else on the scene.

2013

The Riot Grrrl Collection

by Lisa Darms (editor)

A scanned, edited primary-source archive of riot-grrrl zines, drawn from NYU's Fales Library collection. The fanzines themselves, reproduced.

2015

Hunger Makes Me a Modern Girl

by Carrie Brownstein

Sleater-Kinney's Carrie Brownstein on growing up in Redmond, finding the Olympia scene, and what it cost to be in one of the best American rock bands.

2015

Girl in a Band

by Kim Gordon

Kim Gordon's memoir, written in the aftermath of her divorce from Thurston Moore and the end of Sonic Youth. Cold, precise, and unforgiving.

2020

No Icon

by Kim Gordon

Rizzoli photo-and-essay book covering Gordon's art, music, and fashion work. The visual companion to Girl in a Band.

2022

This Woman's Work: Essays on Music

by Kim Gordon and Sinead Gleeson (editors)

Essays from Anne Enright, Margo Jefferson, Megan Jasper, and others on women in music, edited by Gordon and Sinead Gleeson. A corrective to every "women in rock" anthology.

2019

Face It

by Debbie Harry

Debbie Harry's memoir with Sylvie Simmons. Pre-Blondie waitressing, Bowery survival, heroin, Andy Warhol, and Blondie's second act. Illustrated by fan art.

2014

Clothes, Clothes, Clothes. Music, Music, Music. Boys, Boys, Boys.

by Viv Albertine

The Slits' guitarist on London punk, single motherhood, illness, and starting over. Possibly the best-written memoir to come out of UK punk.

2018

To Throw Away Unopened

by Viv Albertine

Albertine's second memoir, mostly about her mother's death and her own rage. Sharper and angrier than the first book.

2004

Cinderella's Big Score: Women of the Punk and Indie Underground

by Maria Raha

Survey covering the Runaways, the Slits, the Raincoats, L7, Sleater-Kinney, Le Tigre, and the women left out of "women in rock" lists. Published by Seal Press.

1999

Pretty in Punk: Girls' Gender Resistance in a Boys' Subculture

by Lauraine Leblanc

Rutgers University Press ethnography on girls in 90s punk subcultures. The academic foundation for almost every later book on gender and punk.

2019

Revenge of the She-Punks: A Feminist Music History

by Vivien Goldman

Vivien Goldman, who wrote about the Slits in real time in the 70s, on women in punk from Poly Styrene to Pussy Riot. The journalist's long view.

2018

Punk Women: 40 Years of Musicians Who Built Punk Rock

by David A. Ensminger

Oral-history interview collection with women across four decades of US punk, including Alice Bag, Penelope Houston, and Cinder Block.

2010

Neon Angel: A Memoir of a Runaway

by Cherie Currie

Cherie Currie of the Runaways on Kim Fowley, statutory abuse, and surviving the band. Source material for the 2010 Runaways film.

2011

Violence Girl: East L.A. Rage to Hollywood Stage, A Chicana Punk Story

by Alice Bag

Alicia Armendariz of The Bags on growing up in East LA and fronting one of the first-wave LA punk bands. Essential context for Decline of Western Civilization.

ARTICLES, ESSAYS, PIECES THAT GOT IT RIGHT ———

ARTICLES

  • The Story of Feminist Punk in 33 Songs2016

    Pitchfork staff, with essay by Vivien Goldman — Pitchfork

    Pitchfork's 33-song playlist and accompanying Vivien Goldman essay tracing feminist punk from Patti Smith to Sleater-Kinney. The canon-setter for the 2010s.

  • Turning the Tables: The 150 Greatest Albums Made By Women2017

    NPR Music staff — NPR

    NPR's 2017 list, the launch of the multi-year Turning the Tables project that put women at the center of the popular-music canon. Patti Smith's Horses, Sleater-Kinney, and Hole all charted.

  • Bikini Kill frontwoman Kathleen Hanna looks back in the memoir "Rebel Girl"2024

    NPR staff — NPR

    NPR's interview with Kathleen Hanna on the release of her 2024 memoir. The clearest on-record version of the Bikini Kill origin story.

  • "My Life as a Feminist Punk": An Interview With Kathleen Hanna2024

    Ms. Magazine staff — Ms. Magazine

    Ms. Magazine — the publication that named the third wave — interviewing the woman who fronted it about her book. The frame matters.

  • Kathleen Hanna Won't Save You, So Stop Asking Her To2024

    CREEM staff — CREEM

    The relaunched CREEM doing the long Hanna interview the original CREEM never got around to. On parasocial fandom and the limits of icon-making.

  • Kim Gordon: The Godmother of Grunge on Feminism in Rock2014

    Rolling Stone staff — Rolling Stone

    Kim Gordon's post-Sonic Youth, post-divorce sit-down on feminism, ideology, and being uncomfortable with the labels people stick on her.

  • Debbie Harry on a life like no other: "I have a stubborn will to survive"2019

    NME staff — NME

    NME's interview around the Face It memoir, the one where Harry talks about being a feminist without ever using the word as branding.

  • Viv Albertine of the Slits: a life in clothes, music and boys2014

    The Guardian staff — The Guardian

    Guardian feature around the publication of Albertine's memoir. The clearest in-print summary of why the Slits matter from someone who was there.

  • Silent For Years, A Riot Grrrl Steps Back To The Mic2013

    NPR staff — NPR

    NPR's review of The Punk Singer at release. Sets up Hanna's Lyme disease silence and the documentary's aftermath.

  • Dis-Content Farm: An Interview With Vivien Goldman2019

    The Quietus staff — The Quietus

    Vivien Goldman — Slits chronicler turned NYU professor — interviewed about Revenge of the She-Punks. The long-view perspective.

  • Punk rock band Big Joanie on their album "Back Home"2022

    NPR staff — NPR

    NPR sitting down with Stephanie Phillips of Big Joanie about the Black feminist lineage from the Raincoats to now.

  • Big Joanie Rewrite the Rules of Punk, Blending Joy and Resistance On The Defining "Back Home"2022

    AFROPUNK staff — AFROPUNK

    AFROPUNK's feature on Back Home, framing Big Joanie inside the broader Black punk lineage from Bad Brains and Poly Styrene forward.

  • Bikini Kill star and riot grrrl legend Kathleen Hanna: in her own words2024

    The Big Issue staff — The Big Issue

    Hanna interviewed by the UK street paper around Rebel Girl. The conversation about class, poverty, and punk that the US press tends to skip.
