FOOTAGE.

The movies that caught it on tape. The documentaries that finally got around to saying her name.

FILMS

1980

Times Square

dir. Allan Moyle

Two teenage girls run away to form a band called the Sleez Sisters in pre-Disneyfied Times Square. The first film to put a riot-girl friendship at the center.

1982

Ladies and Gentlemen, the Fabulous Stains

dir. Lou Adler

Diane Lane at 15 fronting a fictional punk band, with actual Sex Pistols and Clash members in the supporting cast. Bootlegged on VHS for a decade and finally restored.

1982

Smithereens

dir. Susan Seidelman

First American independent film accepted into competition at Cannes. Susan Berman as a Jersey girl trying to scam her way into the East Village scene.

1985

Desperately Seeking Susan

dir. Susan Seidelman

Madonna at the peak of her early punk-adjacent moment, in a film directed by Smithereens' Susan Seidelman. The downtown-NY look in mainstream multiplexes.

1987

Border Radio

dir. Allison Anders, Dean Lent, Kurt Voss

Black-and-white road movie starring Chris D., John Doe, Dave Alvin, and Iris Berry. Allison Anders' debut, the bridge from LA punk to American indie film.

2013

We Are the Best! (Vi är bäst!)

dir. Lukas Moodysson

Three thirteen-year-old girls in 1982 Stockholm form a punk band because they hate gym class. The most accurate film ever made about being in a teenage band.

2007

Itty Bitty Titty Committee

dir. Jamie Babbit

Le Tigre soundtracked queer feminist comedy about a radical activist collective. Babbit (But I'm a Cheerleader) directing actual riot grrrl politics into a rom-com.

2010

The Runaways

dir. Floria Sigismondi

Kristen Stewart as Joan Jett, Dakota Fanning as Cherie Currie, Michael Shannon as Kim Fowley. Cherie Currie consulted; Joan Jett executive-produced.

2013

CBGB

dir. Randall Miller

Mixed reviews, but Malin Akerman as Debbie Harry and Mickey Sumner as Patti Smith are the reasons to sit through it.

2001

Hedwig and the Angry Inch

dir. John Cameron Mitchell

Stage musical adapted by its creator into a film about a genderqueer East German punk rocker. Stephen Trask's songs are the closest a movie has come to actual glam-punk.

2015

Green Room

dir. Jeremy Saulnier

A touring punk band — including Alia Shawkat's bassist — gets trapped in a neo-Nazi venue. The most clear-eyed film ever made about underground touring economics.

2017

Turn It Around: The Story of East Bay Punk

dir. Corbett Redford

Documentary in genre but narrative in feel, narrated by Iggy Pop. Spitboy, the Yeastie Girlz, and Frightwig get the credit Bay Area punk usually gives the boys.

1996

Foxfire

dir. Annette Haywood-Carter

Angelina Jolie as a drifter who radicalizes four teenage girls into a gang. Adjacent to punk in soundtrack and stance, the kind of film We Are the Best! is in dialogue with.

1993

Mi Vida Loca

dir. Allison Anders

Echo Park girl-gang film from the writer/director who came up through the LA punk scene. The female counterpoint to all the boys-in-the-hood films of the same year.

1992

Gas Food Lodging

dir. Allison Anders

Fairuza Balk and Ione Skye as sisters in a New Mexico truck-stop town. J Mascis scored it; the film carries the same DIY weather as Anders' punk-scene roots.

DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE ———

DOCUMENTARIES

2013

The Punk Singer

on Kathleen Hanna

dir. Sini Anderson

The film that broke Hanna's long silence about her Lyme disease and traced Bikini Kill → Le Tigre → The Julie Ruin. The riot-grrrl documentary of record.

2005
DOC

Don't Need You: The Herstory of Riot Grrrl

on Riot grrrl

dir. Kerri Koch

Talking-head history that interviewed the scene's founders while the memory was still raw — Kathleen Hanna, Allison Wolfe, Tobi Vail, Corin Tucker, Sharon Cheslow.

1981

The Decline of Western Civilization

on Los Angeles punk, 1979–1980

dir. Penelope Spheeris

X, Catholic Discipline, Alice Bag fronting the Bags — Spheeris filmed the LA scene with women treated as equals. Banned by the LAPD chief from screening in the city.

1988

The Decline of Western Civilization Part II: The Metal Years

on Sunset Strip metal, late 1980s

dir. Penelope Spheeris

Not punk strictly but essential context — Spheeris turning the same lens on the boys' club hair-metal scene and exposing the absurdity that punk had spent ten years fighting.

1998

The Decline of Western Civilization Part III

on LA homeless gutter punks

dir. Penelope Spheeris

Spheeris' decade-late finale, following kids on the street. Final Conflict's Cheryl Hennessy and a generation of girls who got chewed up by the scene.

2018

Bad Reputation

on Joan Jett

dir. Kevin Kerslake

Sundance-premiered, Jett-authorized doc tracing the Runaways through the Blackhearts. Iggy Pop, Debbie Harry, Kathleen Hanna, Miley Cyrus on the record.

2008

Patti Smith: Dream of Life

on Patti Smith

dir. Steven Sebring

Filmed over eleven years. Sebring travels with Smith through tours, family time, and visits to the graves of Mapplethorpe and her husband. Closest you'll get inside.

2016
DOC

Grrrl Love and Revolution: Riot Grrrl NYC

on Riot Grrrl NYC chapter

dir. Abby Moser

Moser filmed the New York chapter in 1993 and shelved the footage for 20 years. The east coast counterpoint to the Olympia-heavy riot grrrl story.

2015
DOC

Lost Grrrls: Riot Grrrl in Los Angeles

on Riot Grrrl LA

dir. Vega Darling

The Los Angeles chapter's overlooked history — Emily's Sassy Lime, Mecca Normal, the underground show circuit around Jabberjaw.

2021

Poly Styrene: I Am a Cliché

on Poly Styrene

dir. Celeste Bell, Paul Sng

Co-directed by her daughter Celeste Bell, with narration from Ruth Negga. The story of the mixed-race teen who screamed "Oh Bondage Up Yours!" and what it cost her.

2011

Her Aim Is True

on Photographer Jini Dellaccio

dir. Karen Whitehead

Profile of the Pacific Northwest rock photographer who shot the Who and the Sonics in the 60s while in her 50s, then mentored the Seattle scene that produced Mia Zapata.

2009
DOC

Gossip — All My Lovers

on The Gossip and Beth Ditto

dir. Manuel Fenn

German doc following Beth Ditto and the Gossip on the Music for Men tour. Fat-positive queer punk hitting European arenas the year before "Heavy Cross" went platinum.

2007

Punk's Not Dead

on American punk, 1977–2007

dir. Susan Dynner

Thirty-year survey of US punk; the sections on the Donnas, Distillers, and Lunachicks are some of the few on-camera interviews from those bands in that era.

2022
DOC

Kim Gordon: This Woman's Work

on Kim Gordon

dir. Yuri Goncharov

Documentary tied to Gordon's essay collection, tracking her solo work and the visual-art career that ran in parallel with Sonic Youth.

2019
DOC

Bikini Kill: It's So Hard to Live

on Bikini Kill reunion tour

dir. It Books / Bikini Kill archives

Concert/tour film capturing the 2019 reunion shows that sold out venues triple the size of anything Bikini Kill played in the 90s. Proof of how the audience caught up.

2016

L7: Pretend We're Dead

on L7

dir. Sarah Price

Twenty years of L7 home-video footage edited by Sarah Price (American Movie). Donita Sparks throwing her tampon at the Reading Festival crowd, recovered on Hi8.
